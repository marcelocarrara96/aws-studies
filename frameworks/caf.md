# CAF, Cloud Adoption Framework

> AWS's guide for organizations moving to the cloud. A roadmap for people, process, and technology.

---

## 🧠 What it is
The AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF) provides guidance and best practices to help organizations build a comprehensive approach to cloud adoption. It groups capabilities into six perspectives covering the entire organization, not just IT.

---

## 🔑 The 6 perspectives

### 👔 Business
Ensures cloud investments align with business outcomes.
- Business value realization
- Business risk management
- Cloud financial management (FinOps)
- **Stakeholders:** CFO, CEO, business owners

### 👥 People
Bridges technology and business, focuses on culture and skills.
- Cloud fluency
- Workforce transformation
- Organizational change management
- **Stakeholders:** CIO, HR, team leads

### 🏛️ Governance
Orchestrates cloud initiatives while managing risk.
- Program and portfolio management
- Cloud financial management
- Risk management and compliance
- **Stakeholders:** CTO, architects, risk officers

### 🔧 Platform
Builds an enterprise-grade, scalable hybrid cloud platform.
- Platform architecture
- Data architecture
- CI/CD pipelines and automation
- **Stakeholders:** CTO, solutions architects, engineers

### 🔒 Security
Achieves the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.
- Identity and access management
- Vulnerability management
- Infrastructure protection
- **Stakeholders:** CISO, security engineers

### ⚙️ Operations
Ensures cloud services are delivered at the agreed level.
- Observability
- Event management
- Incident and problem management
- **Stakeholders:** VP Infrastructure, Site Reliability Engineers

## 🚀 Transformation domains

| Domain | Focus |
|---|---|
| Technology | Migrating and modernizing infrastructure and apps |
| Process | Digitizing, automating, and optimizing operations |
| Organization | Realigning teams, roles and skills for cloud |
| Product | Creating new value propositions enabled by cloud |

---

## 📈 Cloud adoption journey phases

```
Envision → Align → Launch → Scale
```

| Phase | Goal |
|---|---|
| Envision | Identify business outcomes and build the case |
| Align | Identify gaps across all 6 perspectives |
| Launch | Run pilots and prove value in production |
| Scale | Expand pilots, retire legacy, optimize continuously |

---

## 🏛️ Architect's view

CAF is not a technical framework, it's an **organizational change framework**. As a Solutions Architect, you'll use it to:

- Align stakeholders across the business before migration
- Identify capability gaps (skills, processes, governance) early
- Build the business case for cloud investment
- Structure a phased migration roadmap

> 💡 Think of CAF as the *strategy layer* on top of the Well-Architected Framework (WAF), which is the *technical layer*.

---

## ❓ Exam tips (SAA-C03)

- CAF has **6 perspectives**, know all six names and their stakeholders
- **Business, People, Governance** = business perspectives
- **Platform, Security, Operations** = technical perspectives
- CAF is about **people and process** as much as technology
- Often paired with **Migration Strategies (7 Rs)** in exam scenarios
